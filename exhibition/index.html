Empowering Women: A Celebration of Women in Art
curated by Fiona Yzabelle Gumaroy
In “Empowering Women: A Celebration of Women in Art”, the exhibition explores the importance of bodily autonomy, body integrity, and the idea of a person’s uncoerced choice. This idea is illuminated through the variety of artworks that is showcased: from the lens of cyberfeminism to destigmatizing sex work to a dating simulator that replicates interactions with men that view women as conquests, and finally, the use of AI to manipulate a female character’s physical appearance. While it is not limited to the experiences that women solely go through, the exhibition prioritizes talking and highlighting the works of female presenting people and/or the works focusing female presenting people.

1991: The Cyberfeminist Manifesto For The 21st Century by VNS Matrix

This art project displays an empowering Cyberfeminist Manifesto. It was first exhibited on a 6 by 3 billboard, where the manifesto sits on the shoulders and neck of a bald female presenting person, who resembles the body language of the sculpture “The Thinker”.

2016-2019: The Game: The Game by Angela Washko

The art project manifests as a dating simulator with multiple choice options, where the player interacts with men at a bar. They treat the player as a competition trying to convince the player into a sexual encounter.

2024: XHAIRYMUTANTX by Holly Herndon and Mat Dryhurst

This art project consists of using AI models to alter Holly Herndon’s physical appearance of being a female character with red bangs, contrasted with blue eyes, and having white skin. When submitted a short description, the AI generates a picture that it think best resembles the given description.

2020: E-Viction by veilmachine

E-viction is a virtual exhibition that existed for 12 hours where it gave people the opportunity to interact with sex workers and artists that created sex live and art on camera.
